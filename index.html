ABOUT THE GAME

Deceased Nucleus takes inspiration from the game Exploding Kittens. This game incorporates science terms into the game for you to learn while playing

This game is a turn based card game rotating clockwise where you do your best to avoid getting a Deceased Nucleus

HOW TO PLAY

START OF GAME

Click the READY button to start the game, you will be dealt 4 starting cards for the current game

First player to move is random

Each player has 1 starting lysosome

GAME MECHANICS

Draw a card for every turn, read every card's effect and use it wisely to turn the tide of the game to your advantage

Lysosomes and Smooth ER can save you

Avoid the Dead Nucleus card

No Lysosome = Game Over

How to actually play

Click the Middle card to draw a card

Rotation is clockwise (Left to Right)

You are unable to use a card when its not your turn

GAME TIPS

Collect and Select all your cards, strategize when you want to use it to defeat your enemies

Only shuffle if you have a Defender Card

Do not overuse your shuffle and draw

LEARN ABOUT THE CARDS

There are 6 types of cards Defend, Attack, Shuffle, Thief, Seek and Game Over Card

GAME OVER

The Deceased Nucleus marks the end of your chances of winning

Does not work if user has Lysosome or Smooth ER

DEFEND

Both the Lysosome and Smooth ER can defend you from the Deceased Nucleus

As long as you have at least one you wont lose

ATTACK

Mitochondria forces an opponent to draw and increase chance of drawing a nucleus.

Cell Membrane blocks an opponent from drawing a card

Useful when next card is a defender card or a Dead Nucleus

THIEF

Ribosome makes you steal a card from an opponent requires 2 ribosomes to work

Rough ER user draws one card from the bottom of the deck

SEEK

The Cytosol enables user to see through 3 random cards of an opponent

SHUFFLE

Golgi Apparatus shuffles the deck and user draws 1 card

Centriole cuts the deck in half, upper and lower half of deck are now switched

Cytoskeleton Gives a random card to every opponent
